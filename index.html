Biography of Western/American Music

Introduction

America has only been around for about 250 years. Over that short period of time, we've come to develop our own style of music. What we call "Western Music". But what is "Western Music" really? What makes music Western? Listen to the following five clips. Are they examples of Western Music? If not, what are they?







What do you think? The pieces are listed below respectively:

  1. Selection from Rodeo by Copland (American)
  2. Hungarian Dance No.5 by Brahms (Hungarian)
  3. Mazurka in D major, Op. 33, No. 2 by Chopin (Polish)
  4. Selection from American in Paris by Gershwin (American)
  5. Fanfare for the Common Man by Copland (American)

Could you tell which ones were American? Yes, most likely. But can you identify what makes it American? Where did these traits originate?
