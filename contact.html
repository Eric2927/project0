    • I can only offer a simple summary for now. I'll consider making another website later. Note: The following content is drastically simplified.
      Composer Time Period Contribution Most Famous Works
      Johann Sebastian Bach (1685-1750) Baroque Bach was a master of counterpoint and harmony. He could arrange the most complex individual voices together into a magnificent work. He is regarded as one of the greatest composers of all time.
      • St. Matthew Passion
      • The Well Tempered Clavier (Books 1 and 2)
      • Brandenburg Concertos
      • Mass in B minor
      George Frederick Handel (1685-1759) Baroque One of the greatest Baroque composers, Handel was known for his operas, oratorios, and concertos for organ. He also introduced many newly invented instruments into his works, laying down the foundation for their future uses in orchestral compositions.
      • Messiah
      • Water Music
      • Ode for St. Cecilia's Day
      • The Harmonius Blacksmith
      Joseph Haydn (1732-1809) Classical Haydn was known for his work in the transition from Baroque style into Classical style and the development of the symphony and string quartets, two key compositional genres of the classical period. Because of that, he is often referred to as "The Father of the Symphony" and "The Father of the String Quartet". He also had a tremendous influence on Mozart and Beethoven, two other pivotal composers in music history.
      • Symphony No.104, "London"
      • String Quartets Op.20 "Sun Quartets"
      • The Creation
      Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart (1756-1791) Classical Mozart was a prodigious composer who was known for his symphonies, operas, and concertos. He is one of the biggest names in music history, known for his ability to compose masterpieces with ease. If Copland's music is "The Sound of American Music", then Mozart's music is "The Sound of the Classical Era".
      • Symphony No.40 in G minor
      • Don Giovanni
      • The Marriage of Figaro
      • Piano Concerto No.24
      • String Quartet in C Major
      Ludwig van Beethoven (1770-1827) Classical/Romantic Beethoven revolutionized music. He was the crucial link between the classical era in music and the romantic era, known for his dramatic and intense music. Many at the time were not prepared for the forcefulness of his music. He's also known for introducing programmatic music in one of his later works.
      • Symphony No.3 in Eb Major "Eroica"
      • Symphony No.5 in C minor "Fate"
      • Symphony No.6 in F major "Pastoral"
      • Symphony No.9 in D minor "Choral"
      • Sonata in C minor "Pathetique"
      • Sonata in C# minor "Moonlight"
  • Who is your favorite composer of all time?
    • I would have to say I am a huge fan of Beethoven. His violent and emotional music has a strong influence on me, and I can often relate to it.
